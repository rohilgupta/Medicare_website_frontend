Climate change and its impact on health



Climate change is having a significant impact on global health. The rise in average temperatures, sea level rise, extreme weather events, and other climate-related phenomena are leading to an increase in the frequency and severity of health problems Here are some key ways climate change affects health:

1. Air pollution: Climate change contributes to air pollution, which can exacerbate respiratory conditions such as asthma and increase the risk of cardiovascular disease.

2. Extreme weather events: Climate change increases the frequency and severity of extreme weather events such as hurricanes, floods, and droughts, which can result in injuries, displacement, and mental health impacts.

3. Vector-borne diseases: Changes in temperature and rainfall patterns can lead to the spread of vector-borne diseases such as malaria, dengue fever, and Lyme disease.

4. Food security: Climate change affects crop yields and can lead to food shortages and malnutrition, which can have significant impacts on health.

5. Mental health: Climate change can cause stress, anxiety, and trauma, particularly for those who are displaced or affected by extreme weather events.
